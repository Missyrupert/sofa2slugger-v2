Fight IQ

The language of boxing

Offensive Tools

Jab

What

A quick, straight punch with your lead hand. The first punch you learn and the last one you master.

Why

Sets everything up. Measures distance, disrupts rhythm, creates openings.

How

Flick a wet towel. Power comes from the snap. Extend fast, retract faster.

Cross

What

A straight power punch with your rear hand, rotating hips and shoulders through.

Why

Your hammer. The jab probes, the cross punishes.

How

Push a heavy door with your whole body. Back foot pivots, hip turns, shoulder follows.

Hook

What

A curved punch in a horizontal arc, targeting the side of head or body.

Why

Attacks angles straight punches can't reach. Comes from the blind spot.

How

Slam a door shut. Arm bent at 90°, power from hips snapping round.

Uppercut

What

A vertical punch driven upward, targeting chin or body at close range.

Why

When the straight line is blocked, you go under.

How

Drop hand, bend knees, drive up. Standing up fast with fist in the way.

Defensive Structure

Stance

What

Your foundational position. Feet placement, weight distribution, hand position.

Why

Everything starts here. Weak stance means weak punches.

How

Feet shoulder-width, staggered. Knees soft. Like standing on a surfboard.

Guard

What

Defensive hand position protecting head and body when not throwing.

Why

Defence keeps you fighting. Your guard is your seatbelt.

How

Hands by cheekbones, elbows tucked. Chin down, eyes up.

Slip

What

Head movement off centre line, letting punches pass by.

Why

Blocking absorbs damage. Slipping avoids it entirely.

How

Bend at waist, not knees. Two inches is a mile.

Roll

What

Bend and rotate to let hooks pass over your head.

Why

Rolling takes you under and back up ready to fire.

How

Duck under a clothesline. A U-shape, not straight down-and-up.

Ring Generalship

Footwork

What

How you move. Stepping, pivoting, cutting angles while balanced.

Why

Feet set up everything. In range to land, out of range to get hit.

How

Push, don't step. Never cross feet. Sliding on ice.

Range

What

Distance between you and target. Long, mid, close—different tools.

Why

Punches only work at specific distances.

How

Jab is your measuring stick. Fully extended is long range.
